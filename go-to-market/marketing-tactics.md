## Tactic: Co-Author a Concept Paper with a Vendor-Neutral Leader

**Source:** [Justin Norden (Qualified Health CEO) + Jennifer Goldsack (DiMe CEO) in Healthcare IT News](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/our-co-founder-and-ceo-justin-norden-md-share-7465226253948706816-7OzW/)
**Spotted:** May 2026

### What it is
Find a credible executive or thought leader at an organization with no vendor affiliation — an association, a health system, a policy org — and co-write a concept paper or article together. Publish it somewhere your buyers read.

The vendor-neutral co-author gives the piece credibility that a solo vendor byline can't have. It signals that real practitioners believe in the ideas, not just the company selling something.

### Why it works
- Positions Keywell as a strategic thinker, not just a product vendor
- The co-author's network distributes the content for you
- Thought leadership pieces have longer shelf life than ads or social posts
- Opens a relationship with an influential org that may become a partner or reference
- Can be tied to a working session or roundtable to generate the material (and additional relationships) simultaneously

### The Qualified Health example
Justin Norden (CEO, Qualified Health — a Keywell competitor) co-authored with Jennifer Goldsack, CEO of the Digital Medicine Society (DiMe), a vendor-neutral nonprofit. Published in Healthcare IT News. The article addressed why AI pilots fail — up to 80% don't advance beyond pilot phase, 95% never achieve ROI — and offered four operational learnings from a closed-door working session with 12 health systems and 6 AI vendors.

The tactic did two things at once: established Qualified Health as a trusted voice on AI implementation, and gave DiMe a platform to share research. Both sides win.

### How Keywell could use this
Potential co-author profiles:
- CEO/policy lead at a state Medicaid association (NASHP, MESC community)
- Leader at a health system with no AI vendor contract (credible practitioner voice)
- Executive at a public sector-focused nonprofit or think tank
- A PCG or similar partner who can speak to implementation outcomes

Potential topics aligned to Keywell's positioning:
- Why government health agencies' AI pilots stall (and what changes the outcome)
- The hidden cost of AI SaaS in public sector health — a governance and procurement perspective
- What "owning your AI" actually means for a health plan's data team

### To execute
1. Identify the right co-author — vendor-neutral, respected in the target buyer's world
2. Pitch a narrow, contrarian, or data-backed topic they'd want their name on
3. Consider hosting a small working session to generate the raw material (also a BD move)
4. Place in a publication buyers read: Health Affairs, Healthcare IT News, StatNews, Modern Healthcare, NEJM Catalyst

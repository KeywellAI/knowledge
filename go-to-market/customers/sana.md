## Case Study

### Keywell integrated enrollment and claims data to risk-stratify members, surface the patients who needed care coordination most, and give case managers a complete view of each member's history.

Sana, an innovative health benefits provider that lets small employers self-insure using stop-loss coverage, had extensive enrollment and claims data but struggled with disparate sources and ad hoc analytics. The fragmentation made it hard to identify high-risk members, enroll them in the right care management programs, and demonstrate value to the small-business clients who bear much of the premium cost.

Keywell built a data integration and analytics strategy to connect the dots between member data, risk assessment, care coordination, and outcomes. The team integrated outputs from a procedure risk grouper to sharpen risk assessment and enriched the data with clinical classification software to better segment the member population. Keywell then developed intuitive patient profiles and clinical dashboards so case managers could see a complete picture of each member's health status — including the ability to review why a patient was flagged and to pull their claims history on demand. To put guidance directly in the care team's workflow, Keywell integrated with MCG (Sana's clinical guidelines vendor) to surface intervention recommendations, with all data integrated into Salesforce, Sana's CRM system of record.

### Outcome

Sana's case management team saved **2–3 hours per patient** on clinical care coordination activities. The team gained a reliable, data-driven list of members for targeted outreach, the ability to quickly understand each member's risk drivers and claims history, and greater confidence in resource allocation and their long-term data architecture — improving their ability to show clear value to small-business clients while laying the groundwork for future analytics.

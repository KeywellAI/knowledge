# Buyer Personas

Last updated: 2026-06-05
Owner: Marketing / GTM

Keywell's primary buyer personas for health plan and HHS sales. Use these to tailor outreach, qualify opportunities, and map pain to Keywell's solutions.

---

## Analytics & BI / Data Science Leader

**Typical titles:**
- Director, Analytics Technology
- Sr. Director / Head of Data & Analytics
- Head of Claims & Service BI
- VP, Health Outcomes & Analytics

**What they own:**
Data infrastructure, reporting pipelines, and BI outputs. Responsible for the analytics that drive plan performance measurement, forecasting, and operational decision-making.

**Key pains:**
- Many workflows that feed reporting are still manual, creating bottlenecks and error risk
- Data and tooling are fragmented across systems, making unified analytics difficult
- Facing an AI mandate from leadership with little or no data science headcount to execute it

**Why Keywell:**
Keywell installs AI scaffolding directly inside the customer's existing Databricks environment — so automation runs where their data already lives, without requiring a dedicated data science team to operate it. Fragmented pipelines become governed, auditable workflows, and the organization can execute on its AI mandate without hiring.

## Clinical & Quality Leader

**Typical titles:**
- VP, Enterprise Population Health
- Director, Clinical Data & HEDIS Technical Ops
- Director, Clinical Operations & Case Management
- VP, Clinical Operations

**What they own:**
Clinical performance and operations programs — STARS/HEDIS, care gap closure, prior authorization, and care management. These programs carry CMS audit exposure and significant revenue consequences, making accuracy and timeliness non-negotiable.

**Key pains:**
- AI pilots have failed to move past proof-of-concept into production workflows
- Prior authorization and appeals processes remain heavily manual
- Outreach operations (chase lists, next best action) are imprecise and inefficient

**Why Keywell:**
Keywell replaces failed AI pilots with governed workflow automation that actually runs — deployed inside the plan's own environment so clinical data never leaves, audits are defensible, and care gap and prior auth workflows operate at scale without manual intervention.

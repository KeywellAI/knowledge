# Case Study: FindHelp — NMDOH Food Access & Health Impact Modeling

Last updated: 2026-06-08

## Client

FindHelp

## Scope

FindHelp partnered with Keywell to evaluate whether proxy NMDOH (non-medical determinants of health) datasets could estimate the health impact of improved access to healthy food.

## Population

- 2.7M+ FindHelp users (food-related search and program data)
- Combined with County Health Rankings, Feeding America survey data, U.S. Census county data, food insecurity measures, and community resource availability across U.S. counties

## Keywell Solution

Keywell cleaned, profiled, normalized, and joined the datasets to compare:
- Food need and program availability
- Diabetes and obesity rates
- Quality-of-life measures and healthy days per month

Keywell created a county-level food intervention opportunity index and trained predictive models to estimate where improved food environments could most influence health outcomes.

## Outcome

In pilot modeling, a **20% improvement in food environment score** was associated with a predicted increase of up to **10% in healthy days per month** — helping identify counties where food access interventions could have the greatest modeled health impact.

## Positioning Variations

Pick the variation based on what the audience needs to hear first. Each leads with a different layer of the message stack.

### Pain-First (Job to be done)
Best for: cold outreach, discovery calls, audiences that feel the pain but haven't named the solution.

> *"AI pilots stall. SaaS contracts pile up. Point solutions multiply the problem they were supposed to solve. Keywell installs the operational scaffolding layer that lets health plans actually run governed, auditable AI workflows — inside their environment, not ours."*

### Proof-First
Best for: technical audiences, Databricks shops, buyers who lead with "how does it work?"

> *"Keywell is Databricks-native AI scaffolding for health plan operations — so automation runs where your data already lives."*

### Differentiator-First
Best for: late-stage buyers comparing vendors, CFO conversations, audiences tired of subscription bloat.

> *"Keywell helps health plans own their intelligence — deploying AI scaffolding in their cloud so automations become an asset, not a subscription."*

## Key Terms

- **AI scaffolding** — what Keywell installs; the operational layer that makes governed AI workflows possible

- **Governed and auditable** — how we describe the operational quality of Keywell workflows
- **Compounding value** — what ownership produces over time vs. recurring SaaS fees
- **Non-SaaS / Non-point solution** — how we contrast with the competition

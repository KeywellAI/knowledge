## Overview

Central Health is a safety-net health system based in Austin, TX. It operates Sendero Health Plans and serves Travis County. Central Health is a Central Health Municipal Health District.

**Scale:**
- ~200,000 Sendero plan members
- ~200,000 Central Health patients
- ~700,000 annual visits

**Infrastructure:** Azure cloud environment (existing). Keywell's proposal is to deploy within their existing Azure + Databricks environment.

## Use Case

ED utilization and readmissions reduction via predictive modeling. Central Health issued RFQ #2605-001 seeking an immediate solution for avoidable ED utilization and preventable hospital admissions/readmissions, with the understanding that the solution will become a foundation for future predictive modeling and AI deployments.

## Key Context

- Central Health is a partner with NMDOH (New Mexico Department of Health) on community initiatives, including a FindHelp partnership.
- They are interested in social factors (SDOH), behavioral health, and complex comorbid populations.
- Their care coordination programs include both Sendero members (plan) and Central Health patients (safety-net system).
- Strong interest in AI governance, NIST AI RMF compliance, and owning/extending the solution over time (not a SaaS subscription model).

## Keywell Proposal Positioning

Keywell proposed its **AI Scaffolding** approach: deploy governed, reusable AI infrastructure inside Central Health's existing Azure environment (approximately two-week deployment), then layer Quickstarts for ED utilization and readmissions prediction on top. Key differentiators emphasized:
- Client ownership — perpetual license, data stays in their environment
- Speed — 2–3x faster than ground-up build
- Team's direct experience with Texas HHSC, Texas Medicaid STAR/STAR Plus populations
- Databricks Intelligence Platform (April 2025 Gartner MQ leader)
- FindHelp connection (Keywell rebuilt FindHelp's data infrastructure)

## THE SOLUTION

So what does Keywell provide exactly? We call it "scaffolding" — it's the operational layer required so AI can actually run in a healthcare environment.

Scaffolding includes:
1. Infrastructure and data structures required, like healthcare data ontologies and full-stack infrastructure.
2. Governance capabilities like model cards, evaluation, and budget management.
3. Security, with user-level SSO audit trails to all agents and resources.

## QUICK-START WORKFLOWS

With Scaffolding deployed in weeks or days, we then deploy QuickStarts focused on real pain points like prior auth, appeals management, document routing, and member complaints. Quickstarts are turnkey point solutions that can be customized to exact customer specifications.

## DATA CONNECTIVITY

Your model doesn't have to move to use it.

One of the critical questions we get is "do we have to move our data into your cloud," and the answer is no — the data should typically stay in place. A unified data layer is what allows compounding value out of this, because every solution built on top of it shares its data with other workflows. No more data silos!

## OUR PROCESS

To put it all together, this timeline shows how this is different from a DIY build or a SaaS platform. The first two weeks are reserved for deploying the scaffolding. After we get the approvals in place we can typically do this in a day or two, but we build in some contingency.

Then, the focus turns to the real use cases and pain points — connecting to the data, building out context and the actual applications required.

Final phases include team training and enablement, as well as validation and evaluation.

What's notable here is that most of this time is NOT spent standing up infrastructure, setting up governance and audit trails and agent permissions and limitations, and LLM connections and APIs. So much more time is spent on customization and adoption, which is where pilots typically fail because when you're building it from scratch, you're out of budget and momentum by the time you get to that point.

## PRICING

[We may take out specific numbers for some customer pitches]

I want to share more about our pricing model, because we think it's advantageous over other options.

So with traditional PMPM and SaaS licensing models, you are paying a flat or PMPM fee to use the software. And if there are AI efficiency gains — which there will be — you won't benefit from those; the vendor will. The value of the open infrastructure model compounds on efficiency, but also because, over time, you can consolidate and integrate point solutions while de-risking the build.

Building from scratch is also an option, and with AI tools out there, it's definitely more appealing than it used to be. But you'll probably end up spending a lot of time building much of the stuff we've already built to deal with governance and infrastructure.

Our pricing is for a perpetual license to the full deployment, which you can build and extend upon with no recurring fees. The value we provide is ongoing maintenance of the platform, budget and optimization monitoring, and ongoing innovation in the form of Quickstarts that you can use to replace point solutions over time. But if you decide to kick us off the project, you'll still have sovereignty over the implementation.

## Important Note on External Frameworks

The CHAI AI Governance Framework (and other external frameworks like NIST AI RMF) are valuable reference materials — but they are written **for healthcare delivery organizations (HDOs) deploying AI**, not for AI vendors or developers like Keywell.

**Keywell should not blindly adopt the CHAI framework.** Instead, use it to:

1. **Understand what your clients need to do** — Keywell's clients are often the HDOs this framework was designed for. Understanding their governance obligations helps Keywell build solutions that fit into client governance programs.
2. **Use it as a sales and positioning tool** — Clients implementing these controls are looking for vendors who understand and support their governance needs. Keywell's built-in governance infrastructure (model cards, audit logging, MLOps, evaluation sandbox) maps directly to what CHAI calls for.
3. **Inform Keywell's own governance** — Some controls are relevant to Keywell internally, especially around responsible AI principles, model documentation, third-party disclosure, and data management.
4. **Adapt selectively** — Where controls are relevant to Keywell's operating context, adopt them thoughtfully. Where they assume an HDO structure (governance committees, patient consent, HIPAA covered entity obligations), translate them to Keywell's context rather than copying wholesale.

Every organization's governance needs are shaped by its specific context: who it serves, what data it touches, what regulatory environment it operates in, and what maturity level it's at. Keywell is a startup AI company, not a 500-bed hospital.

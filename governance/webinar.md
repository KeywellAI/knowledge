## Background and Context

The CHAI AI Governance Playbooks (released 2025–2026) have quickly become the closest thing to an industry standard for health AI governance. They were developed with 150+ contributing organizations and define consensus "must-have" baseline controls for healthcare delivery organizations (HDOs).

There is significant demand for practical guidance on AI governance — clients and prospects are increasingly being asked by their leadership: "What is our AI governance program?" Keywell is positioned to help them answer that question.

Key context for content planning:
- Keywell's architecture directly addresses many CHAI baseline controls (model cards, audit logging, lineage, monitoring, evaluation sandbox)
- CHAI distinguishes between governance of **vendors** (third party management) and governance by **deployers** (the HDO); Keywell operates in both roles
- The framework emphasizes: risk-based approach, build on existing infrastructure, do not create parallel systems
- Agentic AI governance is a major emerging topic — the CHAI playbooks have specific notes on agentic risks in every domain

## Potential Content Topics

1. **The CHAI Framework: What it is and what it requires** — Overview of the 9 domains/subdomains; what "baseline operational controls" means; who it's for
2. **The Shadow AI problem** — Why most organizations are already governing the tip of the iceberg; how to surface what's actually in use
3. **Risk-based governance: working smarter, not harder** — How to tier your governance effort; what requires full review vs. expedited vs. exempt
4. **What your AI vendors should be providing** — Model cards, training restrictions, change notifications, monitoring reporting; what to look for in contracts
5. **Agentic AI governance: the new frontier** — Why agentic AI requires extending governance beyond outputs to actions; irreversibility, autonomy, multi-agent risks
6. **Keywell's approach: governance built in** — How Keywell's platform addresses these requirements; client governance as a feature, not an add-on
7. **Getting started without building from scratch** — Practical first steps: AI inventory, model card for your highest-risk solution, update your NPP, add AI tag to existing incident reporting
